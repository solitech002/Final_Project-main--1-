Roasted Fish and Plantain

Fresh whole fish of the day (Tilapia or Crocker) is seasoned and fried then marinated and broiled in our in-house tomato, onions, sweet bell peppers base sauce. Garnished with a stir-fry veggie medley. Enjoy it with the Mabins spicy dipping sauce.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

2 servings

Tags : Lunch Dinner Desert Food

instructions

step 1

Rinse the fish fillets under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Season the fish generously with salt, pepper, and any desired herbs or spices. with mix of paprika, garlic powder, or herbs like thyme or dill, depending on your preference.

step 2

Heat a non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat and add a drizzle of olive oil or a knob of butter Once the pan is hot, carefully place the seasoned fish fillets into the pan. Cook the fish for about 3-4 minutes on each side, depending on the thickness of the fillets. The fish is done when it flakes easily with a fork and is opaque throughout. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the fish for added flavor (optional).

step 3

Peel the plantains by slicing off both ends and making a lengthwise slit along the skin. Remove the skin and cut the plantains into desired shapes - slices or diagonal cuts work well. Heat oil in a frying pan or skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the plantain slices in the pan, making sure not to overcrowd them. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn golden brown. Remove the plantains from the pan and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
